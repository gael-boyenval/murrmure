# Why Murrmure

## The shift

Teams are adopting AI coding agents fast. But the tools for *working with* those agents haven't caught up. Agents ship work into chat threads, pull requests, and DMs — and humans are left stitching context back together by hand.

## What breaks without Murrmure

- **Chat chaos.** Feedback is scattered across threads, screenshots, and "wait, which version?"
- **Lost context.** The agent forgets what was asked; the reviewer forgets what was answered.
- **No audit trail.** When something ships wrong, nobody can reconstruct who approved it.
- **Humans and agents out of sync.** Work moves faster than the team can review it — so quality and trust slip.

## What Murrmure gives you

| Pain | With Murrmure |
|------|-------------|
| Feedback scattered in chat | One shared session per piece of work |
| "Which version am I looking at?" | A live preview everyone reviews together |
| Approvals lost in DMs | Gates with explicit sign-off |
| "Who approved this?" | A complete, append-only audit trail |
| Re-prompting agents by hand | Handoffs that wake the next agent automatically |

## Who it's for

- **Reviewers & designers** — review the real thing in the browser, comment, approve. Nothing to install.
- **Team leads & PMs** — see what's in flight, gate what ships, and keep a record for compliance.
- **Agent operators & engineers** — connect agents in one paste; build custom workflows in your own repo.
